# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Professional immigrants in Australia navigating a local job market that doesn't match their overseas experience. They have real qualifications and track records, but face structural barriers: unfamiliar ATS conventions, local resume norms, networking gaps, and the psychological weight of starting over in a new country. They use Career Ops alone, at home, often under pressure. They're not browsing — they're working.

## Product Purpose

Career Ops closes the gap between the tools recruiters use and the tools candidates have access to. It provides AI-powered utilities to tailor CVs to specific job descriptions, decode what job ads actually require, map a career path in the Australian market, generate outreach copy, and track applications through a kanban pipeline — all local, all private, no cloud lock-in.

Success looks like: a candidate walks into an interview having submitted a tailored application that reflects their real strengths in language the recruiter expects.

## Brand Personality

Warm, supportive, practical. Professional and trustworthy. Calm without being cold. The tool takes the user's situation seriously without being heavy-handed about it. It never performs excitement it hasn't earned.

## Anti-references

- **Startup hype landing pages** — gradient everything, big bold claims, social proof badges, founder headshots. Feels performative and untrustworthy.
- **Generic SaaS dashboards** — bland blue-on-white, identical card grids, hero metrics, AI-palette color choices. Looks like it was generated, not designed.
- **Overly clinical / enterprise cold** — pure utilitarian, zero personality, feels like HR software from 2015. Users are already stressed; the tool shouldn't add to it.
- **LinkedIn / Indeed aesthetic** — bloated, corporate, impersonal. The exact environment users are trying to navigate — Career Ops should feel like a counterpoint to it.
- Specifically: no purple gradients, no bouncy animations, nothing that signals "SaaS marketing site."

## Design Principles

1. **One action per screen.** Every view presents one obvious primary action. The user never has to hunt for what to do next.
2. **Wayfinding always.** The user knows where they are, what step they're on, and how to get back. No dead ends, no mystery states.
3. **Finished, not fragmented.** Every state is designed — empty states, loading states, errors. Nothing looks unfinished or broken.
4. **Breathing room is the product.** Users arrive stressed. Generous spacing and quiet layouts are a feature, not a luxury.
5. **Earn trust through precision.** The tool builds confidence by being correct, consistent, and complete — not through visual enthusiasm.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA minimum: 4.5:1 text contrast ratio, full keyboard navigation, screen reader compatible. Reduced motion should be respected via `prefers-reduced-motion`. No time-limited interactions. The tool's users may be navigating in English as a second language — copy should be clear and jargon-free.
